Abstract

Abstract & Keywords

Abstract
150–300 words. Must include: objective, methodology, key findings, and conclusions. Should be a concise, self-contained summary readable independently of the full paper.
Keywords
4–6 keywords. Choose specific, searchable terms that accurately reflect the content. Avoid overly broad or generic terms that don’t differentiate your work.
Structure

Recommended Manuscript Structure

✓
Introduction: Background, research gap, objectives, and significance of the study.
✓
Literature Review: Critical survey of relevant prior work with gaps identified.
✓
Methodology: Research design, data collection, and analysis methods described clearly and concisely.
✓
Results & Discussion: Findings with interpretation and comparison to existing literature.
✓
Conclusion: Summary of contributions, limitations, and future research directions.
✓
References: Minimum 20 references recommended. Any consistent citation style accepted (see below).
References

Citation Style Policy

Flexible Citation Policy: IJMEER accepts any standard academic citation style. The only requirement is consistency throughout the manuscript — do not mix citation styles within a single paper.

IJMEER accepts any standard academic citation style including APA, MLA, Chicago, Harvard, Vancouver, IEEE, and others. Authors may use any consistent citation format they are comfortable with. The only requirement is consistency throughout the manuscript — each reference must follow the same chosen format.

Ethics

Ethical Requirements

Authorship Criteria
All listed authors must have made substantial intellectual contributions. Ghost authorship and guest authorship are strictly not permitted.
Conflict of Interest
Authors must disclose all financial, professional, or personal relationships that could influence their work or interpretation of results.
Data Availability
Authors are encouraged to share datasets via a public repository and include a data availability statement in the manuscript.
Human / Animal Studies
Research involving human participants or animals must have appropriate ethics committee approval. A copy of the approval certificate is required at submission.

Process

Step-by-Step Submission Guide

1
Prepare Your Manuscript
Ensure your manuscript follows the formatting guidelines above: Times New Roman 12 pt, 1.5 line spacing, single column. Run a plagiarism check — similarity must be below 15%.

Publishing Agreement

Understanding your rights, obligations, and IJMEER’s commitments as your publisher.

Author-Friendly Policy: Authors retain copyright of their work. IJMEER publishes under an open-access Creative Commons licence — your research remains freely available to all readers worldwide.
Copyright Retained by Author
Authors retain full copyright of their work and grant IJMEER a non-exclusive licence to publish. You remain the copyright owner.
Creative Commons Licence
All articles published under CC BY 4.0. Anyone may share, adapt, and build upon your work, provided appropriate credit is given to you as the author.
Originality Declaration
Authors declare the work is original, not previously published, and not under review elsewhere. Simultaneous submission to multiple journals is not permitted.
Post-Publication Corrections
Minor corrections (typos, formatting) can be requested within 30 days of publication. For significant errors, a formal erratum process applies. Contact the editorial team promptly.

Maximising Post-Publication Impact

Publishing is just the beginning. Use these strategies to maximise the reach, visibility, and citation impact of your research.

Share on Academic Networks
Upload your paper to ResearchGate, Academia.edu, SSRN, and your institutional repository. Upload the post-print (accepted manuscript) to comply with open-access policies.
Promote on Social Media
Share your article link on Twitter/X, LinkedIn, and Facebook. Use relevant academic hashtags, tag co-authors and your institution. A compelling abstract thread can drive significant traffic.
Update Your Scholar Profile
Add your published article to your Google Scholar profile, ORCID, Scopus Author ID, and PubMed (if applicable). Keep your profiles updated to improve discoverability.
Engage with Your Community
Present at conferences, seminars, and webinars. Respond to comments and citations. Engage with researchers who cite your work to build collaborations and extend your impact.
Cite Your Own Prior Work
Appropriately self-cite your previous publications where genuinely relevant. Build a coherent body of work that positions you as a recognized expert in your field.
Monitor Your Article Metrics
Track views, downloads, and citation counts via Google Scholar and ResearchGate. Use Altmetric to measure online attention. Share milestones to sustain momentum and visibility.
