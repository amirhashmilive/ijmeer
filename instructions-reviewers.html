Instructions for Peer Reviewers

A comprehensive guide to help reviewers evaluate manuscripts fairly, constructively, and efficiently for IJMEER.

The Reviewer's Role

Peer reviewers are independent subject experts who assess whether a submitted manuscript is scientifically sound, original, and appropriate for publication in IJMEER. Your role is to advise the Editor-in-Chief — the final publication decision rests with the Editor.

IJMEER uses double-blind peer review: author identity is hidden from you, and your identity is hidden from the author. This promotes unbiased evaluation.

Before You Accept a Review

When you receive a review invitation, check the following:

✓
The manuscript is within your area of expertise
✓
You have no conflicts of interest with the authors or research
✓
You can meet the review deadline (typically 2–3 weeks)
✓
You can provide a thorough, unbiased review
If you have a conflict of interest (e.g., you know the author, have a competing publication, or financial ties), decline the invitation and notify the editor immediately.

How to Evaluate a Manuscript

Read the abstract and conclusions first. Ask: Is this within IJMEER's scope? Does it appear novel and significant? If clearly unsuitable, notify the editor promptly rather than completing a full review.
Read the full manuscript carefully. Take notes on: (a) originality and novelty; (b) clarity of research questions/hypotheses; (c) appropriateness of methodology; (d) reliability of results; (e) validity of conclusions; (f) quality of writing and figures; (g) completeness of references.
Your report should include: (1) Summary (2–3 sentences on the study's aims and approach); (2) Major concerns — issues that must be addressed; (3) Minor concerns — suggestions for improvement; (4) Recommendation (Accept / Minor Revision / Major Revision / Reject). Be specific, constructive, and evidence-based in all comments.
A good review is: specific (cite page/line numbers); constructive (suggest improvements, not just criticise); balanced (acknowledge strengths); respectful (professional tone); objective (based on science, not personal preference); timely (submitted by deadline).

Recommendation Guide

Accept — Minor language/formatting corrections only; scientific content is sound and complete.
Minor Revision — A few specific, addressable improvements needed. No new experiments or major restructuring required.
Major Revision — Significant issues with methodology, analysis, or framing. Re-review after revision may be needed.
Reject — Fatal flaws in methodology, not novel, out of scope, or ethical violations. Revision cannot rescue the manuscript.
