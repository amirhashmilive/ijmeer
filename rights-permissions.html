Rights & Permissions

How to reproduce, adapt, translate, or commercially use content published in IJMEER under our open access license.

All IJMEER articles are published under CC BY 4.0. Most uses are permitted without contacting us — simply provide proper attribution as specified below.

What You Can Do Without Asking

📖
Read & Download — Free access to all PDF and HTML articles with no account required.
🔗
Share — Share article links and PDFs via email, social media, WhatsApp, or any channel.
✏️
Adapt & Remix — Translate, adapt, or build upon the work, even for commercial purposes, with attribution.
🏛️
Institutional Deposit — Deposit in any institutional or subject repository without embargo.
🎓
Teaching Use — Use in course packs, lecture materials, and educational resources.
💼
Commercial Use — Incorporate into commercial products or services (with attribution and license notice).

How to Attribute IJMEER Content

When reusing IJMEER content, include the following attribution elements:

Example citation:

Smith, J., & Patel, A. (2025). Title of the Article. International Journal of Multidisciplinary Explication and Emerging Research (IJMEER), 1(2), 1–15. https://www.ijmeer.com. Licensed under CC BY 4.0.

For figures or tables from IJMEER articles, add: "Reproduced from [citation] under CC BY 4.0 license."

Permission Requests

Most uses are covered by CC BY 4.0 and require no additional permission. However, if you require:

  • A specific commercial license beyond CC BY 4.0 scope
  • Permission to use content in a way that may conflict with the license
  • Confirmation for legal/institutional compliance purposes
  • High-resolution print-quality files beyond what is publicly available

Please contact us at editor@ijmeer.com with the subject line "Rights & Permissions Request", including the article title and URL, your intended use, and audience/distribution details. We aim to respond within 10 business days.
