🌟 REMEMBERING IBN SINA (AVICENNA) | 980–1037 CE

Ibn Sina, known in the West as Avicenna, was one of the most influential polymaths in human history. His contributions spanned medicine, philosophy, astronomy, chemistry, and mathematics — the epitome of multidisciplinary scholarship.

Key Achievements:
📖 "The Canon of Medicine" (Al-Qanun fi al-Tibb) — used as the standard medical textbook in Europe and the Islamic world for over 600 years
🔬 Pioneered the concept of infectious diseases and quarantine
💡 Significant contributions to Aristotelian philosophy
📐 Advances in mathematics and natural sciences

Ibn Sina's legacy reminds us that the greatest breakthroughs come from crossing disciplinary boundaries — the very principle that drives IJMEER.

"The knowledge of anything, since all things have causes, is not acquired or complete unless it is known by its causes." — Ibn Sina

🌐 Explore multidisciplinary research at IJMEER: www.ijmeer.com
