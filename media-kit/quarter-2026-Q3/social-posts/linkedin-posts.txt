🌟 Honouring the Legacy of Ibn Sina (Avicenna) | 980–1037 CE

Ibn Sina, known in the West as Avicenna, was a Persian polymath whose contributions to medicine, philosophy, astronomy, and mathematics remain foundational to modern scholarship.

His magnum opus, "The Canon of Medicine" (Al-Qanun fi al-Tibb), served as a standard medical reference across Europe and the Islamic world for over six centuries — a testament to the power of multidisciplinary scholarship.

At IJMEER, we draw inspiration from scholars like Ibn Sina who bridged disciplines and advanced human knowledge across boundaries.

📖 Explore multidisciplinary research: https://www.ijmeer.com/archive.html
