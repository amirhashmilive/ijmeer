Publishing Agreement

The IJMEER Publishing Agreement outlines the terms of publication, the license granted, and the rights retained by authors.

Authors retain copyright of their work. By publishing with IJMEER, you grant us a license to publish and distribute your article — you do not transfer copyright ownership.

License Grant

Upon acceptance of your manuscript, you grant IJMEER (published by Meer Foundation) a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license to publish, reproduce, distribute, and communicate the article in all formats and media, including electronic distribution via the internet and relevant databases.

Your article will be published under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0) license, allowing all readers to freely use, share, and adapt the work with proper attribution.

Author Warranties

By submitting a manuscript and signing the publishing agreement, you warrant that:

✓
You are the author or authorised representative of all co-authors
✓
The manuscript is original and has not been published elsewhere
✓
The manuscript is not currently under review at another journal
✓
All necessary permissions for third-party material have been obtained
✓
The manuscript does not infringe any copyright, trademark, or other intellectual property rights
✓
All research ethics requirements have been fulfilled and are disclosed
✓
All authors have reviewed and approved the final submitted version
✓
All funding sources and conflicts of interest are disclosed

Rights Retained by Authors

As author, you retain:

✓
Full copyright ownership of your article
✓
Right to share the article on personal/institutional websites
✓
Right to deposit in institutional repositories
✓
Right to use in teaching and internal training
✓
Right to include in a thesis or dissertation
✓
Right to share on ResearchGate, Academia.edu, and similar platforms
✓
Moral rights under applicable copyright law

Agreement Process

1
Receive Acceptance Letter
After peer review, you receive an official acceptance letter from the Editor-in-Chief.
2
Review & Sign Agreement
The publishing agreement is sent for digital signature via email. Review all terms carefully before signing.
3
Payment & Publication
After signing the agreement and paying the APC, your article proceeds to publication.
