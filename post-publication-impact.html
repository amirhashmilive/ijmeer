Post Publication Impact

Publication is just the beginning. Here's how to maximise the visibility, reach, and citation impact of your IJMEER article.

Strategy

Amplify Your Research Impact

Researchers who actively promote their work receive significantly more citations and downloads.

Share on Social Media
Share your article on LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Facebook, and ResearchGate. Use hashtags relevant to your field. Tag institutions and co-authors. A single viral post can generate hundreds of downloads.
Upload to ResearchGate & Academia
Upload the final published PDF to ResearchGate and Academia.edu. Add all co-author profiles. These platforms dramatically increase your article's discoverability and allow direct engagement with researchers.
Set Up Google Scholar Profile
Create or update your Google Scholar profile and link your IJMEER article. Monitor citations automatically. Share your Scholar profile in email signatures and on your institutional webpage.
Track Altmetrics
Beyond citations, track online attention: news mentions, blog posts, Wikipedia references, policy documents, and social media shares. Altmetric scores are increasingly valued by institutions and funders.
Use in Teaching & Conferences
Cite your published work in conference presentations, teaching materials, and grant applications. Each mention increases awareness and potential for further citation by your audience.
Engage with Media
For socially significant research, consider issuing a press release to science journalists. Write a lay summary blog post. Journalists looking for experts regularly search Google Scholar for recent publications.

Self-Archiving & Open Sharing

Under IJMEER's CC BY 4.0 Open Access license, you may freely share your published article:

Your institution's repository — Deposit the final published PDF immediately; no embargo.
Preprint servers — arXiv, SSRN, bioRxiv, etc. Post preprints before or after publication.
Personal / lab website — Embed the PDF or link to the IJMEER page from your personal website.
Email sharing — Share the article link directly with colleagues, collaborators, and interested researchers.
