Detailed Profile 
Dr. Nusrat Ali Hashmi stands as a distinguished figure in the Indian legal academic landscape, embodying the rare combination of a senior High Court advocate, a seasoned academician, and an institutional leader. With academic qualifications that include 
B.Com
., LL.B., LL.M., M.Phil., and a Ph.D. in Law, she has built a career spanning over two decades, marked by significant contributions to legal education, research, and practice. Her recent authoritative publications on India's new criminal laws—the Bharatiya 
Sakshya
 
Adhiniyam
 (BSA), Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS), and Bharatiya Nagarik Suraksha Sanhita (BNSS)—have become essential coursework in law institutions across India, cementing her legacy as a leading voice in contemporary criminal law jurisprudence.
Academic Foundation
Dr. Hashmi earned her Doctor of Philosophy in Law from Barkatullah University, Bhopal in 2013, a milestone that solidified her commitment to advanced legal research. Her academic journey began with a Bachelor of Commerce, followed by a Bachelor of Laws, Master of Laws, and Master of Philosophy in Law. Additionally, she holds a Post Graduate Diploma in Computer Applications (PGDCA) and an Advanced Certificate in Web Application, reflecting her interdisciplinary approach and recognition of the growing intersection between law and technology.
Current Professional Roles
Presently, Dr. Hashmi serves as the Editor-in-Chief of the IJMEER Journal, where she oversees the publication of scholarly research and ensures academic rigor in the peer-review process. As a legal advisor to the Meer Foundation, she provides strategic legal guidance on matters ranging from constitutional interpretation to human rights 
advocacy. Her practice as a Senior Advocate at the Mumbai High Court places her at the frontline of Indian jurisprudence, where she argues complex commercial, constitutional, and criminal matters.
Dr. Hashmi previously held the position of Principal at the Shivajirao S. Jondhle Institute of Law and Research in Mumbai. In this capacity, she was responsible for institutional leadership, curriculum enhancement, faculty management, and the organization of moot courts, academic events, and legal workshops. Her administrative acumen shaped the institution's academic culture, fostering an environment where students are encouraged to engage with both theoretical and practical dimensions of law.
Previous Academic Affiliations
Dr. Hashmi's academic pedigree includes a former professorship at The Government Law College, Mumbai, an institution founded in 1855 and recognized as the oldest law school in Asia. Serving on the faculty of such a historic institution speaks to her scholarly standing and her connection to India's legal heritage. She also served as a former professor at the D. Y. Patil University, Mumbai, and as the former Chief Editor of the MATS University Law Journal (MULJ), where she played a pivotal role in shaping legal discourse through published scholarship.
Earlier in her career, Dr. Hashmi held the position of Principal at Oriental College of Law and served as Head of Department at both MATS University and ITM University. Her experience as an Assistant Professor and Guest Faculty across leading law colleges, combined with her roles as Advisor and Coordinator in academic and legal training committees, demonstrates a consistent commitment to nurturing the next generation of legal professionals.
Authoritative Publications on India's New Criminal Laws
Dr. Hashmi has made a landmark contribution to Indian legal literature with her authoritative series of books on the three new criminal laws enacted by the Parliament of India in 2023. These publications have been widely adopted as essential coursework in law institutions across the country, serving as primary reference materials for students, legal practitioners, and judicial officers navigating the transition from the colonial-era penal codes to the new Bharatiya framework.
Her book "New Criminal Laws: BHARATIYA SAKSHYA ADHINIYAM (BSA) with Comparative Table of the New Act with the Old Act" (published May 2025) provides a comprehensive analysis of the new evidence law that replaces the Indian Evidence Act of 1872. The inclusion of comparative tables allows readers to understand the precise changes between the old and new provisions, facilitating a smoother transition for legal practitioners trained under the previous regime.
Her companion volume "New Criminal Laws: BHARATIYA NYAYA SANHITA (BNS) with Comparative Table of the New Act with the Old Act" (published May 2025) examines the substantive criminal code that replaces the Indian Penal Code of 1860. This work 
analyzes
 the new definitions of offenses, revised punishments, and newly criminalized conduct under the BNS, providing critical insights into the evolving nature of criminal liability in India.
Her third volume, "New Criminal Laws: BHARATIYA NAGARIK SURKSHA SANHITA (BNSS) with Comparative Table of the New Act with the Old Act" (published May 2025), addresses the procedural framework that replaces the Code of Criminal Procedure of 1973. This work covers arrest procedures, bail provisions, trial processes, and the newly introduced timelines for judicial decisions, making it an indispensable resource for criminal law practitioners.
The comparative table format across all three volumes is particularly significant, as it enables legal professionals to quickly locate corresponding provisions between the old and new laws, understand substantive and procedural changes, and adapt their practice accordingly. Law institutions across India have incorporated these books into their curricula for criminal law courses, recognizing their pedagogical value in teaching the transition from colonial to post-colonial criminal justice frameworks.
Other Major Publications
In 2021, Dr. Hashmi authored "The Law of Fraud and Misrepresentation in India," a comprehensive text that examines the legal frameworks governing fraudulent conduct and misrepresentation within the Indian legal system. This publication serves as a valuable resource for legal practitioners, scholars, and students seeking to understand the nuances of liability, remedies, and evidentiary standards in cases of commercial deception.
She has also authored "The Intersection of Law and Artificial Intelligence: Exploring Legal Challenges and Ethical Questions" (June 2025), co-authored with Sayed Athar Ali Hashmi, addressing the emerging legal and ethical challenges posed by artificial intelligence technologies, including liability for AI-generated harm, data protection, algorithmic bias, and regulatory frameworks for autonomous systems.
Her scholarly output includes over 25 research papers published in refereed journals, addressing critical areas of law including cyber laws, fraud, consumer rights, judicial reform, violence against women, constitutional remedies, and legal services for the poor. Her article "Justice beyond Death: The Compassionate Appointment Rule as a Constitutional Bridge to Mitigate Hardships Faced by Families of Deceased Employees" (December 2020) examines the constitutional dimensions of employment benefits for families of deceased government employees.
National and International Recognition
Dr. Hashmi's expertise has been recognized beyond national borders. She was invited as a delegate to the International Conference of Jurists in New Delhi in 2010, an event graced by the presence of the Vice-President of India as Chief Guest. Such invitations reflect her standing in the legal community and her contributions to jurisprudential discourse on an international platform.
Throughout her career, she has accumulated a substantial number of academic awards at both national and international levels, though the full breadth of these 
honors
 speaks to a career defined by excellence and peer recognition.
ResearchGate Presence and Scholarly Metrics
Dr. Hashmi maintains an active presence on ResearchGate, the professional network for scientists and researchers, where her research has received attention from the global scholarly community. Her research interests encompass criminal law, evidence law, procedural law, artificial intelligence and law, fraud and misrepresentation, women's rights, constitutional remedies, and legal services for marginalized communities.
Professional Development and Continuing Education
Dr. Hashmi's commitment to lifelong learning is evident through her active participation in workshops and conferences covering a wide range of legal subjects. She has engaged with topics including 
cyber crime
, environmental law, human rights, the Right to Information Act (RTI), and women and child rights. Her attendance at programs organized by the University Grants Commission (UGC), UNICEF, and various international jurist conferences underscores her dedication to staying abreast of emerging legal developments and global best practices.
Professional Summary and Core Competencies
As an experienced legal academic and consultant with a Ph.D. in Law, Dr. Hashmi maintains a strong record in teaching, research, and legal advisory roles. Her proven expertise spans commercial law, constitutional law, human rights, cyber laws, artificial intelligence and law, criminal laws, evidence law, and criminal procedure. She is known for impactful publications, curriculum development, and leadership roles within academic institutions.
Her key skills include commercial and constitutional law analysis, legal research and policy analysis, academic leadership, contract and compliance advisory, public speaking and legal writing, and curriculum design with moot court management. She actively contributes to legal reforms, seminars, and policy dialogues, driven by a commitment to legal education and professional ethics.
Languages and Communication
Dr. Hashmi is proficient in English, Hindi, and Urdu, enabling her to engage with diverse legal communities and client populations across India. Her multilingual capability enhances her effectiveness as an advocate, educator, and researcher, allowing her to access legal materials and communicate judgments across linguistic boundaries.
Legacy and Continuing Impact
Dr. Nusrat Ali Hashmi represents a model of integrated legal professionalism—one where scholarship informs practice, where teaching shapes advocacy, and where institutional leadership creates opportunities for others to excel. Her trajectory from doctoral research to senior advocacy and academic administration illustrates a career built on intellectual rigor, ethical commitment, and service to the legal profession.
Her most enduring legacy may be her authoritative trilogy on India's new criminal laws—the BSA, BNS, and BNSS with comparative tables. As law institutions across India adopt these works as essential coursework, Dr. Hashmi's scholarship is shaping the understanding of an entire generation of law students, legal practitioners, and judicial officers navigating the most significant transformation in India's criminal justice system since independence. Through her ongoing work as Editor-in-Chief of IJMEER Journal, Senior Advocate at the Mumbai High Court, and author of seminal legal texts, she continues to influence the direction of legal education and jurisprudence in India.

Sayed Amir Mustafa Hashmi, widely known as Amir Hashmi, is a National Award-winning filmmaker, author, singer-songwriter, actor, and social entrepreneur who serves as the President of Meer Foundation and the Founder and Managing Editor of the 
International Journal of Multidisciplinary Explication and Emerging Research (IJMEER). His life's work represents a rare convergence of artistic expression, academic publishing, grassroots activism, and environmental restoration. With over a thousand workshops conducted across India in art, culture, and social development, Hashmi has established himself as a transformative leader whose initiatives align with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and create measurable, real-world impact in rural Chhattisgarh and beyond.
Early Life and Artistic Formation
Born into a family of government officers, Hashmi grew up immersed in the literary traditions of Persian and Urdu poetry inherited from his grandfather, alongside the dynamic energy of the Hindi film industry. This dual inheritance shaped his artistic sensibilities from an early age. He began performing on stage during childhood and developed fluency in multiple languages including English, Hindi, Urdu, Punjabi, and Chhattisgarhi. His formal education includes a Master's degree in Mass Communication with specialization in Film and Television from KTUJM, a six-year Sangeet Visharad diploma in Hindustani Classical Vocals from IKSV, and a brown belt in Karate 
Shitō-ryū
. This diverse training reflects his belief in holistic personal development, integrating intellectual, artistic, and physical disciplines.
National Award-Winning Filmmaker
Hashmi's most distinguished achievement in the arts came through his short film "Mirror of the Clean India," which received a National Film Excellence Award from the Ministry of Information and Broadcasting, Government of India. The film was awarded a Certificate of Excellence jointly by the National Film Development Corporation of India (NFDC) and the Information &amp; Broadcasting Ministry for his participation in the Swachh Bharat Short Film Festival Competition. He also received the Certificate of Excellence for Direction of the same film.
His filmography is characterized by socially conscious storytelling. "Culture of Silence" addresses the urgent issue of human trafficking through social networking platforms. "The Journalist" exposes the realities of forced 
labor
. His silent travel documentary "GANGREL – Across 
The
 Mahanadi River" became highly viral on YouTube, while the musical video album "Shiv Vandana" featuring Kathak dance further demonstrates his versatility as a director. Beyond film, he has directed television commercial advertisements including the Brand 
Ambesdor
 TVC featuring Astha Chaudhary under his production banner Meer Corporation.
Hashmi holds memberships in the Indian Film &amp; Television Directors' Association, the Indian Motion Pictures Producers' Association of India, the Film Screen Writers' Association of India, and is a registered artist with the Indian Council for Cultural 
Relations, Government of India, as well as the Directorate of Culture &amp; Archaeology, Chhattisgarh.
Author and Historical Researcher
As an author, Hashmi published "Johar Gandhi," a scholarly work that highlights the role of India's tribal and women freedom fighters from Chhattisgarh—figures often marginalized in mainstream historical narratives. The book was released by 
Honorable
 Chief Minister Bhupesh Baghel at the Shahid Smarak Bhawan in Raipur on October 2, 2022, Gandhi Jayanti. The distinguished gathering included Ashok Khosla, Vijay Mahajan, Lt. General Arun Sahani, Ms. Aika Chaturvedi, and Dr. Vibha Gupta, underscoring the scholarly and political significance of the work.
Meer Foundation: Empowering Communities, Restoring Nature
Founded in 2011, Meer Foundation operates with the tagline "Empowering Communities, Restoring Nature in Chhattisgarh." Under Hashmi's presidency, the foundation has aligned all its initiatives with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), focusing on clean water access, quality education, holistic health, cultural preservation, and sustainable development. The foundation emphasizes real-time change and result-oriented initiatives, positively impacting millions of lives across rural Chhattisgarh.
Bolti Nadi: The Miracle of Reviving a Dead River
Perhaps Hashmi's most remarkable environmental achievement is the Bolti Nadi (Save River Initiative), launched in 2016. Under this initiative, he personally walked the entire 90-kilometer stretch of the Sakri River, from its origin point in Kariyama Village, District Kabirdham, to its end point in Darhi Village, District Bemetara, where it merges with the Half River. The team conducted detailed research by taking water samples, studying animal and agricultural effects, and documenting historical aspects across all connecting villages. They produced a documentary film chronicling the journey. Through this sustained effort, Hashmi and his team revived the Sakri River, which had been completely dry for nearly 30 years. The initiative continues to work across the river basin to reduce pollution, protect headwaters, and promote water-smart practices and policies, ensuring drinking water for more than 20 million people across the state.
Farmour
: Empowering Rural Women and Eliminating Poverty
Under SDG 1 (No Poverty), Meer Foundation launched 
Farmour
, an initiative focused on empowering rural women and communities for socio-economic transformation. By March 2025, the foundation had reached approximately 1 lakh households across 12 districts in Chhattisgarh, primarily targeting marginalized Dalit and Tribal communities. The foundation supports over 860 Self-Help Groups (SHGs) and 35 block-level federations, promoting high-value crops, organic farming, livestock training, and Farmer 
Producer Organizations (FPOs) that transition women from subsistence farmers to business leaders. Over 3,320 households achieved an annual income exceeding ₹250,000 in the 2024-25 fiscal year.
Yoga Kutumb and Health Initiatives
Aligned with SDG 3 (Good Health and Well-being), Yoga Kutumb integrates the principles of holistic well-being into everyday life. With certified yoga professionals leading the way, the initiative shares India's ancient wisdom and cultural heritage, fostering a global community that embraces the transformative power of yoga.
World Record for Patriotism and National Unity
On August 11, 2019, Hashmi played a pivotal role as a core partner with 
Vasudhaiv
 Foundation in organizing the 'Meri Jaan Tiranga Hai' rally in Raipur. The event featured a 
15-kilometer long
 human chain and a 
tricolor
 flag rally, successfully achieving a world record. The flag covered areas from 
Amapara
 square to Pt. Ravishankar Shukla University campus, with diverse participants including school students, CRPF personnel, police, NGOs, and local citizens.
IJMEER Journal: A New Venture in Academic Publishing
As an extension of Meer Foundation's commitment to education and knowledge dissemination, Hashmi founded the International Journal of Multidisciplinary Explication and Emerging Research (IJMEER) in 2025. IJMEER is a quarterly, open-access, peer-reviewed journal that publishes groundbreaking research across more than 70 academic disciplines, ranging from fundamental sciences and engineering to law, humanities, and social sciences. The journal operates on a double-blind peer review model, with first editorial decisions provided within 4 to 6 weeks and average publication within 8 weeks. With affordable article processing charges of ₹2000 for Indian authors and $75 for international authors, IJMEER democratizes access to academic publishing. The journal's first volume, published in 2026, features articles on India's new criminal laws, mental health awareness among college students in Kerala, sustainable agricultural practices in Punjab, digital literacy in rural Maharashtra, and MSME schemes for women entrepreneurs in Tamil Nadu. Through IJMEER, Hashmi has created a platform that bridges rigorous academic scholarship with the foundation's broader mission of education for sustainable tomorrow.
Educational and Cultural Initiatives
EduSuTo
 (Education for a Sustainable Tomorrow) provides structured certification courses in climate change, carbon markets, and environmental management. The Social Heritage Walk, held annually on World Heritage Day (April 18), invites youth worldwide to visit nearby heritage sites and share their journeys using #LocalHeritage, 
preserving cultural legacy. The Chhattisgarh Tribal Sports Development Program further extends the foundation's reach into community development through athletics.
Continuing Impact and Legacy
With over 1,000 workshops conducted across India in art, culture, and social development, Hashmi continues to inspire and empower communities nationwide. He has been invited as a delegate alongside Cabinet Ministers at the Chhattisgarh Excellence Award, nationally broadcast on CNBC, and his interviews have been featured on All India Radio. His work has been recognized through the "Kala Shikhar Award," the "Pride of Artist Award," and numerous other 
honors
. Through Meer Foundation and IJMEER, Amir Hashmi has built an integrated ecosystem of environmental restoration, women's empowerment, cultural preservation, and academic scholarship that serves as a model for result-oriented philanthropy in contemporary India.

Detailed Profile
Dr. Mary Lou Frank is a distinguished psychologist, mediator, and educator who currently serves as a Part-Time Instructor in Psychology at Middle Georgia State University. With a career spanning over three decades at the intersection of psychology, conflict resolution, and higher education leadership, she brings a rare combination of clinical expertise, academic administration experience, and international mediation training to her teaching and professional practice. Her work bridges the empirical study of human 
behavior
 with the practical art of 
peacemaking
, making her a sought-after educator, mediator, and consultant across multiple continents.
Academic Credentials and Educational Background
Dr. Frank earned her Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Psychology with a specialization in 
Counseling
 Psychology from the College of Natural Sciences at Colorado State University in Fort Collins, Colorado. Her clinical internship was completed at the University of Delaware, providing her with supervised clinical experience in a rigorous academic medical setting.
She holds a Master of Science (M.S.) in Psychology with a specialization in 
Counseling
 Psychology, also from the College of Natural Sciences at Colorado State University. Additionally, she earned a Master of Education (M.Ed.) in 
Counseling
 from the College of Education at Colorado State University, which included additional studies in Marriage 
and Family Therapy, broadening her clinical expertise to include relational and systemic approaches to mental health.
Her undergraduate degree is a Bachelor of Arts (B.A.) in Speech (Communication), earned with Distinction from the College of Arts and Sciences at Colorado State University. This foundation in communication studies informs her approach to mediation, negotiation, and conflict resolution, where effective communication is central to successful outcomes.
Beyond her degrees in psychology and 
counseling
, Dr. Frank has pursued advanced training in dispute resolution at the world's most prestigious institutions. She holds a Certificate from Harvard Advanced Negotiations for Executives at Harvard University, one of the most selective and rigorous executive education programs in negotiation theory and practice. She has also completed coursework leading to designation as an Approved Neutral under Civil Mediation through the Georgia Office of Dispute Resolution, demonstrating her commitment to mastering both the theoretical and practical dimensions of mediation.
Teaching and Academic Appointments at Middle Georgia State University
Dr. Frank has been affiliated with Middle Georgia College and subsequently Middle Georgia State University since 2011. She has served as Adjunct Faculty in Psychology from 2013 to the present, and currently holds the position of Part-Time Instructor. Her teaching is conducted entirely online, and she works individually with each student to find convenient meeting times, demonstrating her student-
centered
 and flexible approach to education.
The courses she teaches reflect the breadth of her clinical and research expertise. PSYC 3265 Abnormal Psychology examines the 
etiology
, diagnosis, and treatment of psychological disorders. PSYC 3365 Theories of 
Counseling
 and Psychotherapy explores major theoretical approaches to therapeutic intervention, including the existential theory for which she is a recognized authority. PSYC 3401 Biological Psychology investigates the neural and physiological bases of 
behavior
, cognition, and emotion.
Teaching and Research Interests
Dr. Frank's teaching and research interests encompass the study of abnormal psychology, psychopathology, biological psychology, neuroscience, 
counseling
 theory and psychotherapy, mediation, conflict resolution, and the dynamics of making peace. Her research has focused on neuroscience and conflict, exploring how brain processes influence and are influenced by interpersonal and intergroup conflict. She has also conducted significant research on existential theory and psychotherapy, mediation and mediation training, and the relationship between psychopathology and conflict.
Previous Academic Leadership at Middle Georgia State University
Prior to her current instructional role, Dr. Frank served in senior academic leadership positions at Middle Georgia State University. From 2011 to 2013, she was Interim Vice President for Academic Affairs, College Dean, and Professor of Psychology, demonstrating her capacity to lead at the highest levels of university administration while maintaining her identity as a teacher-scholar.
Concurrent Academic Appointments
In addition to her role at Middle Georgia State University, Dr. Frank holds multiple concurrent academic appointments that reflect her national and international reputation in conflict resolution and mediation education.
At Brenau University, she has served as Adjunct Faculty in Psychology and in the Business Executive MBA Program in Conflict Resolution since 2013, bringing mediation skills to business leaders and executives.
From 2022 to 2024, Dr. Frank served as Adjunct Faculty in Conflict Resolution at Mykolas 
Romeris
 Law School in Vilnius, Lithuania, contributing to their Advanced Law Degree LL.M. program in Mediation. This international appointment reflects her global recognition as a mediation educator.
At Mercer Law School, she has been an Adjunct Faculty member teaching mediation since 2021, training the next generation of lawyers in dispute resolution skills that are increasingly essential to legal practice.
She has also been a Fellow at the University of Georgia's 
McBee
 Institute for Higher Education since 2010, engaging with cutting-edge research and practice in postsecondary education leadership.
Previous Higher Education Leadership Experience
Dr. Frank's career includes significant leadership roles across multiple institutions of higher education. At Gainesville State College in Gainesville, Georgia, she served as Associate Vice President for Academic Affairs and Professor of Psychology from 2006 to 2010. From 2001 to 2006, she was Dean of Undergraduate Studies and Professor of Psychology at Kennesaw State University. Prior to that, she served as Department Head of the Department of Psychology and Sociology and Professor of Psychology at North Georgia College &amp; State University from 1996 to 2001.
Her academic career began at Clinch Valley College of the University of Virginia (now the University of Virginia at Wise), where she served as Coordinator and Lead Professor, Associate Professor of Psychology from 1992 to 1996, and Interim Assistant Academic Dean from 1993 to 1995. She was also an Affiliate Professor in Clinical Psychology at the University of Virginia's Southwest 
Center
 in Abingdon, Virginia, from 1992 to 1996.
Earlier in her career, at Arizona State University in Tempe from 1988 to 1992, Dr. Frank served as Director of the Eating Disorders Program in the Student Health Service, Co-Coordinator of Graduate Practicum Training, and Adjunct Professor in 
Counseling
 Psychology.
Non-Academic Professional Experience
Beyond academia, Dr. Frank served as Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of the Athens Community Career Academy in Athens, Georgia, from 2010 to 2011, leading workforce development initiatives at the community level. She was also a Mediator for the Board of Regents of the State of Georgia University System from 2001 to 2013, resolving disputes within one of the largest public university systems in the United States.
Mediation Practice and Leadership
Dr. Frank is a Licensed Psychologist in the state of Arizona and a Registered Neutral with the Supreme Court of Georgia. She is the Co-founder of Transforming Mediation, LLC, established in 2015, which provides mediation, mediation training, and consulting services to individuals, organizations, and legal professionals.
Her leadership in the field of dispute resolution is evidenced by her service as President of the International Academy for Dispute Resolution from 2018 to 2020, and she has continued to serve as Chair of the Law School Tournament Committee on the Board of Directors since 2019. She has also served on the Board of Directors of the International Women's Think Tank since 2012.
International Mediation Competition Expertise
Dr. Frank has been selected as an expert assessor at numerous international mediation competitions, recognizing her expertise in evaluating mediation performance and providing constructive feedback to emerging mediators. These include the CDRC Mediation Competition in Vienna, Austria; the International Commercial Mediation Competition (ICC) in Paris, France; the ICDRL First Mediation and Negotiation Competition in Pakistan; and multiple INADR Mediation Competitions held in Tbilisi (Georgia), Krakow (Poland), Bialystok (Poland), Kyiv (Ukraine), Vilnius (Lithuania), Dubai (United Arab Emirates), Bologna (Italy), and Athens (Greece).
Publications and Scholarship
Dr. Frank's recent publications demonstrate her ongoing contribution to the fields of mediation and 
counseling
 psychology. She is the author of "The mind of a peacemaker: The psychology of mediation," now in its 3rd edition (Kendall Hunt, 2023), a comprehensive text that integrates psychological research with mediation practice.
She has contributed a chapter on "Mediation Tournaments" (co-authored with K.K. Frank) to "The Art of Advocacy: A Comprehensive Guide to ADR Competitions," edited by 
H. 
Mahaseth
 and published by Routledge in 2026. She authored "The Psychology of Mediation," published in Spanish in "
Multidisciplines
 &amp; Mediation," edited by Dr. Maria Yolanda De Simone and Dr. Maria Victoria Marun in 2024.
Since 1995, Dr. Frank has been the author of the chapter on "Existential Theory" in every edition of D. Capuzzi and D.R. Gross's widely used textbook "
Counseling
 and psychotherapy: Theories and interventions" (1st through 7th editions, published by Prentice Hall), as well as the corresponding Instructor's Guide and Test Bank.
Professional Affiliations
Dr. Frank holds memberships in the American Psychological Association and the American 
Counseling
 Association, the two premier professional organizations for psychologists and 
counselors
 in the United States. She is a member of the International Council for Online Dispute Resolution and serves as a Board Member of both the International Academy for Dispute Resolution and the International Women's Think Tank.
Awards and Honors
Dr. Frank has received numerous awards recognizing her contributions to mediation, psychology, and higher education leadership. She holds an Honorary Doctorate from the University of Georgia in Tbilisi for her work in mediation, as well as the Who's Who Lifetime Achievement Award.
The Reflective Practice Award, given by the International Academy for Dispute Resolution, has been named in her 
honor
, a rare distinction that reflects her foundational contributions to the field. She has received the Global Mediation Synergy Award from the Ukrainian Academy of Mediation, a Certificate of Appreciation from the Three C's Mediation Group in Nigeria, and the Best Speaker Award from Peace Mindset Ambassadors in Africa.
Her leadership in higher education has been recognized with the Administrative Leadership Award from the Diversity Office at Kennesaw State University, the Outstanding Leadership and Service Award from the Georgia Association for Women in Higher Education, and the Georgia Award for Leadership in Education &amp; Health Care, an award named in her 
honor
 by the Georgia Women's Institute.
The Senate of the State of Georgia issued a commendation for her outstanding service (SR 1389, 2010). She has received the 
Leitalift
 "Trailblazer" Award for Leadership and Public Service from the 
Leitalift
 Foundation, the Outstanding Service Award from Phi Kappa Phi, the "Unsung Hero Award" from non-traditional students at Kennesaw State University, the Outstanding Administrator Award from University College at Kennesaw State University, and the Outstanding Faculty Award from Clinch Valley College of the University of Virginia.
She has also received a commendation for work advancing women's leadership from the 
Honorable
 Kim Campbell, former Prime Minister of Canada, and the Distinguished Service Provider Award in 
Counseling
.
Academic Honorary Societies
Dr. Frank is an elected member of numerous academic 
honor
 societies, reflecting her exceptional scholarly achievements throughout her career. These include Phi Beta Kappa (National Academic Honorary, the oldest and most prestigious academic 
honor
 society in the United States), Phi Kappa Phi (National Academic Honorary), Golden Key Honorary (National Service Honorary), Omicron Delta Kappa (National Leadership Honorary), for which she served as Faculty Advisor, Phi Delta Kappa (National Education Honorary), Pi Gamma Mu (National Social Science Honorary), Alpha Kappa Delta (National Sociology Honorary), for which she was a Charter Member, and Psi Chi (National Psychology Honorary), from which she received the Outstanding Faculty Advisor Award.
She has been listed in multiple Who's Who publications, including Who's Who in America, Who's Who in America's Teachers, Who's Who in American Women, Who's Who in Medicine and Health Care, Who's Who in Science and Engineering, Who's Who in the South and Southeast, and Who's Who in the World.
Legacy and Continuing Impact
Dr. Mary Lou Frank represents a rare integration of clinical psychology, academic leadership, mediation practice, and global peace education. Her work at Middle Georgia State University, her leadership of the International Academy for Dispute Resolution, her scholarly publications on the psychology of mediation, and her service as an expert assessor at competitions across four continents collectively position her as a transformative figure in the field of conflict resolution.

Detailed Profile 
Adv. (Dr.) Ashok Yende is a distinguished legal academician, mediator, and practitioner whose career spans over 44 years across administration, teaching, and the legal profession. Currently serving as Professor and Dean of the ATLAS School of Law at ATLAS 
SkillTech
 University in Mumbai, he brings an unparalleled combination of academic leadership, judicial mediation experience, and international exposure to legal education. As a Mediator empanelled with the Supreme Court of India and the Bombay High Court, and as the Ombudsperson of Maharashtra National Law University (MNLU), Mumbai, Dr. Yende has established himself as a leading figure in alternative dispute resolution (ADR) and legal education reform in India.
Academic Credentials and Professional Training
Dr. Yende holds an impressive array of academic degrees. He earned his LL.B. from Nagpur University, followed by an LL.M. from the University of Mumbai. He completed his Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Law from the University of Pune and subsequently earned a Doctor of Literature (D. Lit.) in Law from CCU University in Malawi, South Africa. This combination of advanced degrees reflects his deep commitment to legal scholarship and his standing as a thought leader in the field.
Beyond his formal degrees, Dr. Yende has pursued executive education at some of the world's most prestigious institutions. He successfully completed a public credential program on "Public Policy Designing" conducted by the Harvard Kennedy School, USA, equipping him with advanced skills in policy formulation and analysis. He also completed the "Entrepreneurship Edge" program of the London Business School, demonstrating his commitment to integrating business acumen with legal practice. 
Additionally, he is a certified Online Dispute Resolution (ODR) professional by SAMA, reflecting his engagement with technology-enabled dispute resolution mechanisms.
Academic Leadership at ATLAS School of Law
As Professor and Dean of the ATLAS School of Law at ATLAS 
SkillTech
 University, Dr. Yende leads a forward-looking institution that is redefining legal education for the next generation of changemakers. The ATLAS School of Law, approved by the Bar Council of India, is part of India's first urban, multidisciplinary university. Under Dr. Yende's leadership, the school offers a skill-based and practice-oriented curriculum that integrates emerging technologies, business strategy, and policy frameworks.
Dr. Yende's vision for the ATLAS School of Law is articulated in the institution's distinctive educational philosophy, which he champions. The school blends law with business, technology, and innovation to prepare graduates for emerging global industries. It emphasizes hands-on learning through internships, live projects, and direct industry engagement with top firms. Students are trained in artificial intelligence, legal tech, and digital tools for modern legal practice, preparing them not just for courtrooms but also for boardrooms, policy think tanks, startups, and global enterprises. The programs offered under his deanship include a 5-year integrated BBA LL.B. (Hons.) and a 1-year LL.M. in Law, Technology &amp; Artificial Intelligence.
Previous Academic Leadership at University of Mumbai
Prior to his current role, Dr. Yende served as Professor and Head of the Department of Law at the University of Mumbai, one of India's most prestigious and oldest universities. During his tenure, he established a Pre-litigation 
Center
 and started an ADR course in 2015, demonstrating his pioneering spirit in integrating dispute resolution mechanisms into formal legal education. He also served as the Founding Director of the Mumbai University Law Academy, further contributing to the professional development of legal scholars and practitioners.
Mediation Practice and Dispute Resolution Expertise
Dr. Yende is an empanelled Mediator with the Supreme Court of India and the Bombay High Court, positions that recognize his expertise and integrity in facilitating the resolution of complex disputes. He has successfully resolved disputes referred by the Bombay High Court and the Maharashtra State Consumer Commission, Mumbai. His practice encompasses a wide range of civil and commercial matters, and he brings to each mediation a deep understanding of both the legal framework and the psychological dynamics of conflict.
He has been appointed as the Lokpal (Ombudsperson) of Maharashtra National Law University, Mumbai, a position that entrusts him with the responsibility of addressing grievances and ensuring accountability within one of India's premier national law 
universities. He serves as the Managing Partner at Yende Legal Associates, where he leads a team of legal professionals providing services in mediation, arbitration, legal consultancy, and online dispute resolution.
Publications and Scholarly Contributions
Dr. Yende has authored seven books on legal subjects, demonstrating his prolific scholarly output.  leads an institution with a rich legacy spanning nearly eight decades. The

Detailed Profile 
Dr. Mohammed Salim Baitullah Khan is a distinguished legal academician, researcher, and practitioner who currently serves as Assistant Professor - Senior Scale at the Presidency School of Law, Presidency University in Bangalore. With over 25 years of combined academic and professional experience, he brings an extraordinarily diverse educational background and a multidisciplinary research portfolio to legal education. His expertise spans Sports Law, Intellectual Property Rights (IPR), Business Law, Constitutional Law, Animal Law, Arbitration, and Mediation, making him a versatile and sought-after scholar in contemporary legal studies.
Academic Credentials and Distinctions
Dr. Khan holds an impressive array of advanced degrees that reflect his commitment to lifelong learning and interdisciplinary scholarship. He earned his Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in Law, demonstrating his capacity for original research at the highest level of legal scholarship. He also holds an honorary Doctorate (Ph.D. HC) from the United States, recognizing his contributions to legal scholarship beyond formal academic requirements.
His Master of Philosophy (M.Phil.) in Law was earned with a Gold Medal, the highest distinction awarded for academic excellence at the postgraduate research level. This achievement reflects his exceptional scholarly capabilities and his mastery of advanced legal research methodologies.
Beyond his law degrees, Dr. Khan holds a Master of Business Administration (MBA) in Operations Management, which provides him with deep understanding of business 
processes, supply chain management, and organizational efficiency. He also holds a Master of Arts (MA) in Rural Development, demonstrating his commitment to understanding the legal and developmental challenges facing rural communities in India and globally. His academic foundation includes a Master of Laws (LL.M.) and a Bachelor of Arts (BA).
This unique combination of legal, managerial, and developmental studies enables Dr. Khan to approach legal problems from multiple disciplinary perspectives, enriching both his research and his teaching. His students benefit from his ability to connect legal principles with business realities, rural development challenges, and operational management frameworks.
Academic Appointment at Presidency University
As Assistant Professor - Senior Scale at the Presidency School of Law, Dr. Khan contributes to a dynamic legal education environment within Presidency University, one of Bangalore's leading private universities. The university holds multiple accreditations and recognitions, and the School of Law offers programs that integrate theoretical legal education with practical training in advocacy, arbitration, and legal research.
In his role, Dr. Khan teaches courses in his areas of specialization, including Sports Law, Intellectual Property Rights, Business Law, and Constitutional Law. His teaching is informed by 25 years of professional experience, which includes significant corporate exposure alongside academic appointments. This blend of corporate and academic backgrounds allows him to bridge the gap between legal theory and business practice, preparing students for careers in corporate legal departments, law firms, arbitration chambers, sports governance bodies, and intellectual property practices.
Research Specializations and Scholarly Interests
Dr. Khan's research interests are broad and contemporary, encompassing Arbitration, Mediation, Constitutional Law, Sports Law, Animal Law, and Intellectual Property Rights. His work addresses some of the most pressing legal issues of our time, from the regulation of sports and the protection of animal welfare to the resolution of commercial disputes through alternative dispute resolution mechanisms and the protection of intellectual property in the digital age.
His specialization in Sports Law examines the legal frameworks governing sports organizations, athlete rights, doping disputes, contract negotiations, broadcasting rights, merchandising agreements, and the role of arbitration in resolving sports-related disputes, including the jurisdiction of the Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS).
His expertise in Animal Law addresses the emerging field of legal protections for animals, including wildlife conservation laws, animal cruelty statutes, the legal status 
of animals as sentient beings, and the intersection of animal law with environmental law, criminal law, and constitutional provisions.
His work in Intellectual Property Rights encompasses patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets, and the protection of traditional knowledge, with particular attention to the challenges posed by digital technologies and artificial intelligence.
Publications and Scholarly Contributions
Dr. Khan has published extensively in peer-reviewed journals, edited volumes, and academic books. His recent publications include a chapter on "A Holistic Model for Digital Resilience: Bridging Cybersecurity, Governance, and Risk Management" in the volume "Building Resilient Digital Ecosystems: Security, Governance, and Transformation" (IGI Global Scientific Publishing, 2026), co-authored with 
Budesh
 Kanwer, Smriti Sethi, K. 
Esakki
 Ammal, Komal Saxena, and

 Shavkatov. This 34-page chapter addresses the integration of cybersecurity frameworks with governance structures and risk management strategies in an increasingly digital world.
He also contributed a chapter on "Artificial Intelligence and the Evolving Landscape of Legal Accountability: Ethical, Legal, and Regulatory Issues" to the volume "Modern Perspectives on Artificial Intelligence and Law" (IGI Global Scientific Publishing, 2025), co-authored with Sheikh Inam Ul Mansoor, Hina Kausar, and Govind Yadav. This 26-page chapter examines the ethical, legal, and regulatory challenges posed by the deployment of artificial intelligence systems, including questions of legal accountability when AI systems cause harm.
Dr. Khan has also published a textbook titled "Constitutional Laws of India" (AG Publishing House, 2023), co-authored with K.P. Vora, S. Chaturvedi, and S. Sarkar. This comprehensive text serves as a foundational resource for students and practitioners seeking to understand the constitutional framework of India, including fundamental rights, directive principles, the structure of government, federalism, and constitutional remedies.
His other scholarly articles address diverse topics including the legal perspective of e-banking fraud in India, the impact of cyber laws in information security management to protect businesses and citizens, recent trends in wildlife smuggling in India, and the refugee problem in relation to citizenship law.

Dr. Khan has made significant contributions to intellectual property through the registration of four patents and two designs. His patented work includes "An Innovative Paradigm in Risk Management in Banking Institutions" (Indian Patent Application No. 202241032666, 2022) and "Blockchain - enabled Intelligent IoT Architecture with AI" (Indian Patent Application No. 202141038370 A, 2021). These patents demonstrate his

Book Editing and Authorship
Dr. Khan has edited five books, contributing to the scholarly infrastructure of legal studies by curating collections of research that advance knowledge in his areas of expertise. He has also authored one textbook on the Constitutional Laws of India, providing a comprehensive resource for students and practitioners.
His forthcoming publications include chapters on "Enhancing Trust and Compliance in HR through Blockchain" in a volume on the Impact of AI and Blockchain Technology in the Growth of Digital HRM (2025), and "At the Edge of Authorship: Moral Rights and the Legal Identity of AI-Generated Creativity" in a volume on The Convergence of IPR, Artificial Intelligence and Cyber Law (2025). He has also edited a volume titled "Understanding Humanity: The Intersections of Social Science and Social Work" (Empyreal Publishing House, 2025) and contributed a chapter on "Role of constitutional provisions in addressing unlawful killings and torture: a comparative study of India and Brazil" to the book "Brazil x India: Human Rights, Fundamental Rights and Labor Law" (2025).

With 25 years of experience, Dr. Khan brings a wealth of practical knowledge to his teaching and research. Dr. Khan is an active arbitrator, mediator, and trainer, bringing dispute resolution skills to both academic and professional settings. His expertise in alternative dispute resolution (ADR) is reflected in his research interests and his practical engagement with arbitration and mediation processes. He has conducted training programs for students and professionals, sharing his knowledge of ADR mechanisms, legal research methodologies, and substantive areas of law including Sports Law and Animal Law.

Dr. Khan's scholarly work has been published by major international academic publishers, including IGI Global Scientific Publishing, which has a global footprint with offices in the United States (Pennsylvania and New York), the United Kingdom (London), and China (Beijing). His publications are available to a worldwide audience, contributing to global legal discourse on artificial intelligence, cyber law, data protection, digital resilience, and the evolving landscape of legal accountability.
Legacy and Continuing Impact
Dr. Mohammed Salim Baitullah Khan represents a new generation of legal academics who bring corporate experience, technological awareness, interdisciplinary training, and global publishing networks to legal education. His Gold Medal in M.Phil., his four patents, his edited volumes, his textbook on Constitutional Law, and his research on emerging fields including AI accountability and blockchain-enabled systems collectively position him as a forward-looking scholar committed to preparing students for the complex legal landscape of the twenty-first century.

Detailed Profile
Dr. Momin Ali serves as an Assistant Professor of English at the Department of Humanities and Sciences, MLR Institute of Technology, Hyderabad, India. With a strong academic foundation from Aligarh Muslim University (AMU) and specialized training from The English and Foreign Languages University (EFLU), Hyderabad, Dr. Ali represents a new generation of language educators who bridge traditional English Language Teaching (ELT) with contemporary applied linguistics, curriculum innovation, and international research collaborations. His scholarly work addresses the intersections of language pedagogy, teacher education, inclusive classrooms, and the role of English in shaping academic progression for rural learners.
Academic Credentials
Dr. Ali earned his Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) in English Language Teaching from the Department of English at Aligarh Muslim University, one of India's most prestigious central universities. His doctoral research contributed original insights to the field of ELT, examining pedagogical practices and learner outcomes in the Indian context. He holds a Bachelor of Arts (BA) in Communicative English (Honours) and a Master of Arts (MA) in English Language Teaching, both from AMU.
He has obtained a Post Graduate Diploma in Linguistics from the Department of Linguistics at AMU, broadening his understanding of language structure, phonetics, syntax, semantics, and sociolinguistics. He also holds a Post Graduate Certificate in the Teaching of English (PGCTE) and a Post Graduate Diploma in the Teaching of English 
(PGDTE) from The English and Foreign Languages University (EFLU), Hyderabad, India's premier institution for English language teacher training.
Current Academic Appointment
Dr. Ali joined MLR Institute of Technology on February 10, 2024, as a Regular Assistant Professor. The institute is approved by AICTE and affiliated with JNTUH, Hyderabad. In this role, he teaches English language and communication skills to engineering students, emphasizing English for Academic Purposes (EAP) and English for Specific Purposes (ESP). His JNTUH Unique ID is 9194-240214-155059.
Areas of Expertise
Dr. Ali's specializations include English Language Teaching (ELT), English for Academic Purposes (EAP), English for Specific Purposes (ESP), Teaching English as a Second Language (TESL), Curriculum Designing and Planning, and Applied Linguistics. This diverse expertise enables him to design effective language programs and address the unique challenges faced by learners from varied linguistic backgrounds.
Research Publications
Dr. Ali has published two research papers in international peer-reviewed journals. His paper titled "Theatre for Teacher Education to Prepare Teachers in an Inclusive Classroom: A Historical Perspective of India and Southeast Asia" examines the use of drama-based pedagogies in teacher preparation programs, exploring how experiential learning through theatre can help teachers develop inclusive classroom strategies.
His second paper, "Is English a nemesis to learners' academic progression? A case study of the rural college entrants in Medinipur," investigates whether English serves as a barrier or enabler of academic success for rural students entering higher education. This research has significant implications for language policy and curriculum design in rural educational contexts.
GIAN Courses
Dr. Ali has completed two Global Initiative of Academic Networks (GIAN) courses approved by the Government of India. The first, "Electronic Literature and Artificial Intelligence: Theory and Practice of Digital Storytelling," explores the intersection of digital humanities, electronic literature, and AI technologies. The second, "Subtitling Screened Dialogue: The Pragmatics of Audio-Visual Translation," addresses linguistic dimensions of subtitling for film, television, and digital media.
State Eligibility Test
Dr. Ali qualified the Telangana State-State Eligibility Test (TS-SET) in Linguistics in July 2019, demonstrating his expertise in the scientific study of language structure, variation, and use.
Professional Practice Beyond Academia
Beyond his academic role, Dr. Ali serves as Director of Collaborations &amp; International Partnerships, establishing international academic linkages and global partnership agreements. He also serves as Managing Editor of the institute's Newsletter. He is a Swachh Bharat Awardee, recognizing his contributions to the Government of India's cleanliness campaign. His work has been published in 
HeadlinesBeyond
, The Wire, and The Times of India. He is the Founder of InstantHelpCo19, an initiative that emerged during the COVID-19 pandemic to provide community assistance.
Professional Development
Dr. Ali regularly attends national and international conferences, seminars, workshops, symposiums, and colloquiums, maintaining engagement with current research in ELT, applied linguistics, and higher education pedagogy. This commitment ensures his teaching remains informed by the latest theoretical insights and empirical findings.
Legacy and Continuing Impact
Dr. Momin Ali represents a model of integrated language education professionalism, where doctoral research informs classroom practice, technological awareness enhances pedagogical effectiveness, and international collaborations enrich institutional capacity. His trajectory from doctoral research at AMU to advanced training at EFLU, from State Eligibility Test qualification to international publications, and from classroom teaching to institutional leadership illustrates a career built on intellectual rigor and pedagogical commitment. Through his ongoing work at MLR Institute of Technology, his research publications, and his international partnership initiatives, Dr. Ali continues to shape English language education in Indian higher education.
