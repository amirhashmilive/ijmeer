 Prof. (Dr.) Jyotirmaya Thakur Required Details Field Information Full Name Prof. (Dr.) Jyotirmaya Thakur Designation Retired Principal; Poet, Author, Editor, Reviewer, Columnist, Academician, Researcher, Translator, Motivational Speaker, Philanthropist, Social Activist Institutional Affiliation Former Principal, Educational Institution, United Kingdom Detailed Profile Prof. (Dr.) Jyotirmaya Thakur is a distinguished poet, author, editor, academician, and social activist with over 34 years of professional experience in education, administration, curriculum development, and literary mentorship. She is a retired Principal who has dedicated her life to the empowerment of students, women, and marginalized communities through education, literature, and humanitarian service. With more than 200 awards to her credit, she is a multi-genre award winner and a globally recognized literary figure. She holds prestigious positions in numerous international literary and humanitarian organizations, exemplifying the ideal that writing is more than just words on paper—it is a means of creating positive change in the world. Academic Background and Professional Expertise Prof. Thakur's academic and professional expertise spans multiple domains. She is a scholar and researcher with deep knowledge of English literature, poetry, creative writing, linguistics, and communication skills. Her areas of specialization include curriculum design and management across CBSE, ICSE, and IGCSE boards, academic administration, educational outreach development, personal development, student empowerment, strategic planning, psychological counseling , creative writing, and public speech training. She is also a certified Grand Reiki Master, Karuna Reiki Master, and HFHE (Holy Fire III Healing) Master, reflecting her holistic approach to personal development and well-being. Her expertise in coaching and counseling has made her a sought-after mentor for both children and adults, helping them build confidence, develop communication skills, and navigate personal and professional challenges. Literary Career and Publications Prof. Thakur is the author of twenty-three books, with many more awaiting publication . Her works have been translated into multiple languages and featured in international anthologies. She serves as a reviewer, columnist, and editor for various literary publications, contributing to the global literary discourse on poetry, prose, and contemporary social issues. Her poetry and prose explore themes of human existence, spirituality, nature, women's empowerment, social justice, peace, and the beauty of everyday moments. She believes that poetry is the art that offers depth in a moment, using the depth of a moment. In the short time it takes to read and contemplate a poem, readers can penetrate to deeper levels of experience and more profound truths. She describes poetry as "a sharpened knife" that cuts mangoes to be eaten, removes dead parts, retains healthy parts for rejuvenation, and alleviates the pain of miseries and despair. Philosophy on Literature and Poetry Prof. Thakur holds that literature and poetry are essential to human life for three fundamental reasons: as a means of expressing emotions and communicating ideas of a subjective nature; to provide a link with intelligent culture that modern technology and trends may be diminishing; and to serve as a vessel of revolt against mainstream society, particularly for marginalized groups. She believes that literature changes human society but is in turn altered by the new society it helps create, making it a dynamic and transformative force. She emphasizes that poets and writers have a responsibility and a purpose to give hope in a world marked by wars, poverty, indifference, absence of empathy, and unresolved issues. Her conscience speaks through language as a means of transport to give clarity and interpretation of her own understanding as a conscientious literary person. International Recognition and Editorial Roles Prof. Thakur has been interviewed by international literary platforms, including OPA International, where she shared her views on poetry, literature, contemporary Indian writing, the impact of technology on literature, and the role of writers in promoting peace and human rights. Her interview, published on November 3, 2020, showcased her profound understanding of literary traditions ranging from The Ramayana, Mahabharata, and Gita to Shakespeare, Tagore, Kafka, Tolstoy, Blake, Wordsworth, and Keats. She holds prestigious positions in many international organizations, both literary and humanitarian. Her work as an editor and reviewer has helped shape the careers of emerging writers and poets across the globe. She is deeply committed to promoting peace, individual liberty, equality, and human rights through her literary and humanitarian activities. Teaching and Mentorship With over 34 years of professional experience, Prof. Thakur has dedicated her career to teaching, training, and mentoring students and adults. She has expertise in teaching English language and literature, communication skills, interactive skills, and coordination skills. She believes that sincerity and passion are of great importance in building good human beings, and that the aim of education is to empower children and instill confidence to face the world with dignity and compassion. She describes herself as an industrious, conscientious, and versatile person who believes in going beyond the textbooks. As a mentor, adviser, and trainer for personal and holistic development, she places great importance on her students learning the skills of communication, critical thinking, and research. She is adaptable, reflective, and a lifelong learner, always trying to learn new ways of improving teaching-learning methods. Humanitarian Work and Social Activism Beyond her literary and academic contributions, Prof. Thakur is a committed philanthropist and social activist. She works with NGOs to contribute to diminishing the physical and mental needs of innocent people affected by conflict, poverty, and displacement. She advocates for peaceful means to promote individual liberty, equality, and human rights, and uses her voice as a poet and writer to champion these causes. She has expressed deep concern about the suffering of innocent people in Gaza, Iraq, Syria, Afghanistan, and elsewhere, and believes that the international community must hold accountable those who perpetuate violence and war. She supports the work of International Peace Councils and the United Nations in promoting global peace and security. Vision for the Future Prof. Thakur is hopeful about the future of humanity. She believes that one day, all people will see each other as equal in dignity and embrace everyone, overcoming differences of ethnicity, religion, nationality, and racism. She draws hope from the COVID-19 pandemic, which taught the world that solidarity and togetherness are essential for survival. She prays each night for all the children of the world, believing that they are the future and that the world will become better if we teach our children how to respect each other's differences. She has great hopes for the younger generation, finding them more politically conscious, aware of environmental issues, and confident in believing in a better world of equality without boundaries and barriers of disparity. Her aspiration is that with their youthful energy and wisdom of technology, they will not only bring the world closer in space but also overcome their differences to bring the world together in peace and harmony. Legacy Prof. (Dr.) Jyotirmaya Thakur represents a rare integration of literary excellence, educational leadership, spiritual wisdom, and humanitarian service. Her 23 books, over 200 awards, international recognition, and decades of dedicated service to education and mentorship collectively position her as a transformative figure in contemporary literature and social activism. Through her poetry, prose, teaching, counseling , and humanitarian work, she continues to inspire and empower individuals across generations and geographies, embodying the belief that while there is life, there is hope.
